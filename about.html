Background

I have been a graduate student in the Peery lab since 2019. I use a number of methods to examine the effects of rising temperatures and variable precipitation on the individual fitness and demographic rates of California spotted owls. I am also using bioacoustic methods to examine the co-existence of forest owl species in the Sierra Nevada.

I graduated with a B.A. from Colorado College in 2018, where I studied the population ecology of Flammulated owls in Pike National Forest. I completed a thesis that examined habitat selection by male Flammulated owls throughout their breeding season, and I kicked off an extensive project to understand the migration patterns of the small owl species. Following graduation, I stayed on with Colorado College's Organismal Biology and Ecology department as a paraprofessional.
